ISAAC NEWTON

Derivative serves a vital role in Newton's laws of motions. During his time, he needed a way to solve rates of change related problems to formulate his laws of motions.

Derivative and integrals make up calculus, derivative is about using approximations and infinities to solve for a rate of change; whereas integral is about using approximations and infinities to solve for areas and volume.

Using the ideas of the Greeks, he was able to discover that the instantaneous rate of change at a certain point is can be found using the average rate of change between that and another point on the graph, while making the distance of the points approximate 0.
