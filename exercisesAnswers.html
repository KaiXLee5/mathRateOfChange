IROC

1.a)

$$\lim_{h->0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0}\frac{(x+h)^2-x^2}{h}$$

= $$\lim_{h->0}\frac{x^2 + 2hx + h^2-x^2}{h}$$

= $$\lim_{h->0}\frac{h(2x +h)}{h}$$

= $$\lim_{h->0}2x+h$$

= $$2x$$

$$x=4, 2(4) = 8$$

b)

$$\lim_{h->0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0}\frac{(x+h)^2-7(x+h)+11-(x^2-7x+11)}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0}\frac{x^2+2xh+h^2-7x-7h+11-x^2+7x-11}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0}\frac{2xh+h^2-7h}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0}\frac{h(2x+h-7)}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0}2x+h-7$$

$$=2x-7$$

$$x=-1, -2(-1)-7= -5$$

c)

$$\lim_{h->0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$

$$\lim_{h->0}\frac{-3(x+h)^3-5(x+h)-(-3x^3-5x)}{h}$$

$$\lim_{h->0}\frac{-3(x^3+3hx^2+3h^2x+h^3)-5x-5h+3x^3+5x}{h}$$

$$\lim_{h->0}\frac{-3x^3-9hx^2-9h^2x-3h^3-5x-5h+3x^3+5x}{h}$$

$$\lim_{h->0}\frac{-9hx^2-9h^2x-3h^3-5h}{h}$$

$$\lim_{h->0}\frac{h(-9x^2-9hx-3h^2-5)}{h}$$

$$\lim_{h->0}-9x^2-9hx-3h^2-5$$

$$=-9x^2-5$$

$$x=1, -9(1^2)-5 = -14$$

2.

IROC $$y_1 = \lim_{h->0}\frac{(x+h)^2-6(x+h)+1-(x^2-6x+1)}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0}\frac{x^2+2hx+h^2-6x-6h+1-x^2+6x-1}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0}\frac{2hx+h^2-6h}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0}2x+h-6$$

IROC $$y_1=2x-6$$


IROC $$y_2 = \lim_{h->0}\frac{-2(x+h)+5-(-2x+5)}{h}$$

$$= \lim_{h->0}\frac{-2x-2h+5+2x-5}{h}$$

$$= \lim_{h->0}\frac{-2h}{h}$$

$$= \lim_{h->0}-2$$

IROC $$y_2=-2$$

This shows that at whichever point of $$y_2$$, the IROC is -2. Therefore, IROC of $$y_1$$ must also be -2.

$$2x_{y1}-6=-2$$

$$x_{y1}=2$$

∴ $$x_{y1} = 2, x_{y2} \in \mathbb{R}$$
