The rate of change (ROC) is the speed at which a variable changes over a specific period of time. - Investopedia

∴ Rate of change $$=\frac{Change in dependent variable}{Change in independent variable}$$

Example:

At x = 1, y is 1.

At x = 2, y is 4.

$$Rate of change = \frac{4-1}{2-1}$$

$$=3$$

If we draw a line between those two points, the gradient of the line will be 3, which is also the rate of change.

Gradient formula:

$$m = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$$
