How does AROC compare to IROC?

Formula of AROC:

$$\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$$

ⓘ

IROC is same as AROC except that the distance of x approaches 0

Based on the graph above, say

$$x_1 = x$$ of point A

$$x_2 = x_1+h = $$ x of point B

$$y_1 = f(x_1)$$

$$y_2 = f(x_1+h) = f(x_2)$$

IROC:

$$\lim_{h->0} \frac{f(x_1+h)-f(x_1)}{x_1+h-x_1}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{x+h-x}$$

IROC of $$y=x^2$$ at x=-3 is -6

Steps in finding the IROC

① Write the equation $$\lim_{h->0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$

② Cancel out the h in denominator with h in the numerator

③ Evaluate the limit.

ⓘ

We can't simply change the h of the equation to 0 during the first stage of the equation as it will yield a zero division error. It is explained in the limits page

Eg: Evaluate the IROC for the following equation at x = -3:

$$f(x) = x^2 + 9$$

①Write the equation

$$\lim_{h->0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0} \frac{2(-3+h)^2-9-(2(-3)^2+9}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0} \frac{2(9-6h+h^2)-9-(2(-3)^2+9}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0} \frac{2(9-6h+h^2)-18}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0} \frac{18-12h+2h^2-18}{h}$$

② Cancel out the denominator h

$$=\lim_{h->0} \frac{2h(h-6)}{h}$$

$$=\lim_{h->0} 2(h-6)$$

③ Evaluate the limit.

$$=\lim_{h->0} 2(h-6)$$

$$=-12$$
